**How to select the limits to use for closure**\

The closure problem for fast reactions between two separate streams arises because we do not know the product of the instantaneous concentrations needed to calculate the average reaction rate.  Turbulent fluctuations in concentration, that is the fluctuations in concentration at a fixed location, require modeling.  The combustion community and later Baldgya and others use the concept of mixture fraction and its variance to represent the fluctuations.  Mixture fraction is a conserved scalar with value 1.0 in stream 1 and 0.0 in stream 2.
