**A long-standing problem**\
Since completing my PhD in 1992 and long before that and a few times since, I have returned to the problem of modeling chemical reactions that are mixing-sensitive.  I had the pleasure to meet and work with John Bourne in the late 1980s and early 1990s and to meet Jerzy Baldyga a few times including in Dublin with Steve Hearn in the late nineties.  Building on early findings by Ed Paul of Merck & Co., Bourne and Baldyga laid a great foundation in the field and developed interesting mathematical models.  

**An equation: The Cauchy-Schwarz Inequality**\
$$\left( \sum_{k=1}^n a_k b_k \right)^2 \leq \left( \sum_{k=1}^n a_k^2 \right) \left( \sum_{k=1}^n b_k^2 \right)$$

**A stoichiometric limit**\
$$f_s = C_{B0} / \left(C_{A0} + C_{B0} \right)$$
